Physical protection Riding Gear

For motorcycle riders, riding gear is very important. This is because this gear is the most basic protection for the rider's life. Even though, depending on the magnitude of the accident, motorcycle gear may not be able to completely prevent injuries, wearing the proper gear can increase the probability of survival as much as possible. Therefore, it is highly recommended that proper riding gear should be worn on every ride no matter what. Below is the gear checklist:


  1. Motorcycle helmets that meet DOT and other standards
  2. Proper eyewear
  3. Riding jackets with protective armor
  4. Motorcycle Riding Gloves
  5. Long, non ripped motorcycle riding pants or jeans
  6. Motorcycle riding shoes or boots

Pre-Ride Checks T-CLOCS

Love your ride. Take care of your motorcycle and your motorcycle will take care of you. It only take a couple minutes to make sure your bike is ready for the road. Use T-CLOCS as your pre-ride checklist:


  • Tires: The tire and wheels section covers the tires, wheels, and the brakes section of the safety check on your bike.
  • Controls: The controls category covers the handlebars, levers, pedal, cables, hoses, and throttle safety check on your bike.
  • Lights: The lights and electric section covers the battery, headlamp, brake lamp, turn signals, switches, mirrors, reflectors, and wiring safety checklist on your motorcycle.
  • Oil: The oils and fluids category covers fluid levels and leaks.
  • Chassis: The chassis and chain category covers the frame, suspension, chain, and fasteners on your bike.
  • Sidestand: The stands section covers your kickstand and the center stand. This is the section that varies from the original TCLOCK checklist, as it is important to have safety while performing bike maintenance as well, so the center stand was added.

Take control of your emotions and calm down emotional control

There are times when it's normal to have some bad times in your life. Perhaps you would like to vent your emotions by riding a motorcycle. There are many ways to vent your emotions, but for your own safety and the safety of others, you should not ride when you are emotionally unstable. This is because emotional changes can affect the subjective judgment of what is happening around you, which in turn can affect your ability to execute decisions. So, please do not ride when you are emotionally unstable.
